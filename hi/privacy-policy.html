Information processed by the apps

QR code content

QR apps process the content contained in the QR code selected or scanned by the user. Depending on the app, this may include contact details, email addresses, event details, map or location text, phone numbers, SMS text, plain text, URLs, or Wi-Fi network information. This QR content is processed locally on the device to show it to the user or to prepare the action chosen by the user.

Camera, photos, media, and local storage

Apps that scan QR codes or provide camera features may use the camera permission to display a live camera preview and scan or capture content. Vanity Mirror: Makeup Camera uses the front camera to show the mirror/camera preview and allows the user to take a photo only when the user presses the capture button. Camera preview, scanned QR content, and photos are processed locally on the device. Photos are not uploaded to our servers.

Where an app lets the user save a photo or file, storage or media permissions may be used only to save, read, modify, or delete content created or selected by the user within the app. We do not use these permissions to access unrelated personal files.

Contacts, calendar, phone, SMS, email, browser, maps, and Wi-Fi actions

Some apps may help the user create a contact, add a calendar event, open the phone dialer, compose an SMS or email, open a URL, open a map/navigation app, or connect to a Wi-Fi network. These actions are initiated by the user and may be completed by Android system apps or third-party apps installed on the device. We do not automatically place calls, send SMS messages, send emails, add contacts, add calendar events, or open external links without user action.

Audio and app preferences

Volume Boost: Sound Booster may process audio settings or playback-related information on the device to provide its sound adjustment functionality. Table Clock and other apps may store local preferences such as display settings, audio settings, or similar configuration choices. These preferences are generally stored locally on the device.

Advertising, analytics, crash reports, and consent information

All listed apps may include Google AdMob, Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, and Google User Messaging Platform. These services may process advertising identifiers such as OAID or Android Advertising ID where available, device and app information, app usage information, crash logs, diagnostic logs, performance data, approximate technical information derived from the device or network, and consent choices.

How we use information

  • To provide the core app functionality selected by the user.
  • To scan QR codes, display decoded content, and prepare user-requested actions.
  • To save photos or app preferences when the user chooses to do so.
  • To show ads and measure ad performance.
  • To request, store, and respect privacy and advertising consent choices.
  • To understand app usage at an aggregated or technical level.
  • To detect crashes, diagnose technical issues, improve stability, and prevent abuse or fraud.
  • To comply with legal, platform, and app store requirements.

Disclosure to third parties and international transfers

Technical, analytics, crash reporting, consent, and advertising data may be processed by Google services and may be transferred to or processed on servers located outside your country or region. This may include advertising identifiers, app usage information, crash logs, diagnostic data, and consent-related information.

Photos, camera preview, and QR content are processed locally by our apps and are not uploaded to our servers. They may be shared with another app only when you choose to perform an action that sends, saves, opens, or transfers that content through the device or another service.

We do not sell your personal data.

Permissions

The permissions requested by each app depend on its functionality. Possible permissions include camera, media/storage, contacts, calendar, phone, SMS, Wi-Fi/network, notification, or audio-related permissions. Permissions are requested only when needed for the relevant feature. You can manage app permissions from your device settings.

Data retention

Information stored locally on your device remains there until you delete it, clear app data, uninstall the app, or use an app feature that deletes it. Data processed by Google services is retained according to Google's applicable retention settings and policies. Crash and analytics data is kept only as long as necessary for diagnostics, security, analytics, and legal or platform compliance.

Children's privacy

Our apps are intended for a general audience unless a specific app store listing says otherwise. We do not knowingly ask children to provide names, email addresses, account details, or similar direct personal information. If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child has provided personal data to us, please contact us so that we can take appropriate action.

Your choices and rights

  • You can grant or deny app permissions in your device settings.
  • You can reset or limit advertising identifiers in your device privacy settings where supported.
  • You can manage consent choices through the consent message shown in the app, where available.
  • You can delete locally saved photos, files, or app data from the app or device settings.
  • Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or request portability of personal data processed about you.

Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information processed by our apps. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure.
